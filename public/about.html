About Let Them Trust

Overview

Let Them Trust is a child-oriented creative, educational and cultural initiative that aims to create a platform for young people to groom and showcase their artistic talent.

Mission

To nurture artistic talent, preserve cultural identity & help develop career pathways for the youth.

Through the years, we have developed our programming to include other arterial elements in the youth’s daily lives. As a cultural and traditional rich nation, Zimbabweans are grounded in family values and norms, however the youth of today are now taking in more external traits and habits through various media. With this in mind, LET THEM Trust incorporates workshops and discussion platforms in its programming; giving participants a voice, lending an ear and providing a thought to how they can circumvent issues that affect them. These workshops cover issues such as HIV/AIDS, Child Labour, Sex (and sex for exchange), Teenage Pregnancies, Loneliness, Children’s Rights, Peer Pressure, Physical and Mental Abuse, Substance Abuse, Zimbabwean Culture & Heritage and so much more.

More Than A Passion

This workshop is tailored for Parents and Artistic Children. This workshop provides an open platform for children to express their desire to pursue a career in the arts to their parents. By being impartial during this workshop, LET THEM facilitators give both the parents and the children facts about the Arts industry, at the same time giving both sides an opportunity to air out their concerns.

Express Yourself

This workshop introduces students to critical and out-side-the-box approach to thinking. The participating students explore music and dance forms that they have never encountered or participated in.

Theatre In Schools

'Theatre in Schools' programme aims to educate youth in schools on various personal and social topics that affect them. The message is delivered in theatrical format (plays)  and is followed by a focus-group discussion with students who are present.

Annual School Arts Festival

The annual School's Arts Festival is a two day event open to every school in Zimbabwe. Participating Schools are able to showcase their work in Poetry, Theatre, Music, Dance, Fine Art, Comedy Arts and Fashions.

Radio and TV Programmes

Radio and TV Programmes production shows is tailored for teenagers. Various educational material is shared with topics ranging from environmental heath, sustainability, cultural heritage, performance and visual arts, creativity and artistic expression to organic gardening. Countrywide cultural, artistic and creative events are covered along with guest appearances by celebrities interact directly with the kids during the phone-in session.

Art Business Training & Career Pathways

Member students take up a three-month Arts Business course. Students choose amongst different courses in disciplines that they would like to pursue within the arts. The Training Courses ends with a Project and Test exercise to evaluate what they have learnt. Members who complete the training courses will be eligible to enter our Career Pathway programme in which they will enter an internship engagement with one of our participating partner organizations.

Youth Camps

LET THEM Youth Camps run annually. The 3 weekend camps is segmented for separate youth groups (10-12/13-15/16-19yr group category) are aimed at leadership development with the Arts as centric ground.

Litter 2 Art (clean-up campaign)

LET THEM Trust mandate is recycling waste. Which is in line with efforts worldwide on global warming. The Litter2Art programme aims at addressing this issue and educating children in a more engaging manner. Participating students are tasked to convert some of the litter they would have collected into visual and purpose art. The top 20 designs are then exhibited, and a winner is selected.

Alumni Discotheque

LET THEM’s fundraising event open to parents who wish to support the Arts In Schools initiative.

Mbira Month

Mbira is a month-long event celebrating mbira in all its facets worldwide every September. Its focus is school children and anything and everything relating to how they can attain a decent mbira education in present day Zimbabwe. Mbira Month aims to engage the general public in as many of its activities as is technically possible. For this reason social media and radio will be major platforms for presenting our activities to the public. Activities on the Mbira Month include a children’s mbira inspired art exhibition, Mbira related national radio broadcasts, performances and traditional children’ games and will be will be regularly posted on relevant Facebook pages and on other social media.
